What is a Business Plan?

Published on July 9, 2026 by Business Expert

A Business Plan is a written document that explains your business goals, products, target customers, and financial plans. It acts as a roadmap for starting and growing a successful business.

A good business plan includes the Executive Summary, Market Analysis, Marketing Strategy, Operations Plan, and Financial Planning.

1. Executive Summary

A business plan is the single most important document a founder will write before launching a company. It is part roadmap, part sales pitch, and part financial model, and it forces you to answer the hard questions before a customer, investor, or bank asks them.

Key takeaway: a plan is a living document, not something you write once. Founders who treat their first draft as final fall behind competitors who keep iterating.

A good business plan guides you through each stage of starting and managing your business. - U.S. Small Business Administration

2. Market Analysis

1. Understanding Your Audience

Before writing financial projections, define who you are selling to. Segment your market by demographics, behavior, and need:

  1. Identify the total addressable market (TAM)
  2. Narrow to your serviceable available market (SAM)
  3. Define your serviceable obtainable market (SOM)
    • Local competitors
    • Regional competitors
    • Online-only competitors
  4. Validate assumptions with real customer interviews

2. Competitive Ranking

Competitors ranked from least to most significant threat:

  1. Small local shops
  2. Regional chains
  3. Established e-commerce sellers
  4. Well-funded startups
  5. The current category leader

3. Key Market Terms

Market Penetration
The target market that buys your product or a competitor's.
Value Proposition
The tangible results a customer gets from using your product.
Customer Acquisition Cost
Sales/marketing spend divided by new customers acquired.
